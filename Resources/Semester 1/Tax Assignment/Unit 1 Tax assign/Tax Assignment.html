1. Income →

i. What is Income ?

→ Income is a tax on an individual or entity's earnings or profits, which varies with respect to the income or profit earned.

ii. Types of Income :

→ There are 3 types of income :

  • Earned Income : It is an income that is direst result of your labour.
  • Unearned Income : It is an income from investment and other sources which is earn related to employment.
  • Casual Income : It is a nonrecurring income that is not likely to occurred again in year.

    // It is an income which is earned by chance.

2. Person →

i. What is Person ?

→ A person is an individual who is subject to taxation and can be considered as a taxpayer.

It includes individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), Association of Persons (AOPs), Body of Individuals (BOIs), and companies.

3. Assessee →

i. What is Assessee ?

→ An assessee is an individual or entity who is liable to pay tax to the government for the income earned or received during a particular financial year.

ii. Type of Assessee :

→ There are 4 types of assessee :

  • Normal assessee : A normal assessee is an individual who liable to pay tax for income earn by him for a particular financial year.
  • Representative assessee : Many time it so happens that an individual is liable to pay tax for income incurred not only by him but also for income incurred by a third party.

    // If the person is minor or the person is a deceased person.

  • Deemed assessee : Deemed assessee is an individual who is put in a position to pay taxes for some other person by the legal authority.
  • Assessee in default : An assessee in default is an individual who has failed to fulfill his legal duty of paying tax to the government.

4. Deemed Assessee →

i. What is deemed assessee ?

→ Deemed assessee is an individual who is considered as an assessee by the legal authority, although they are not the actual assessee.

For example, a legal heir who inherits property from a deceased person may be considered a deemed assessee under certain circumstances.

5. Previous Year or Financial Year →

i. What is Previous Year (Financial Year) ?

→ Previous Year or Financial Year is a period in respect of which a person has to pay tax. It is the year in which the income is earned.

For example, if someone earns income between April 2021 and March 2022, then the financial year would be 2021-2022 and the previous year would be 2020-2021.

6. Assessment Year →

i. What is Assessment Year ?

→ Assessment Year is the following year in which the income is assessed and taxed.

For example, if someone earns income between April 2021 and March 2022, then the financial year would be 2021-2022 and the assessment year would be 2022-2023.

// So assessment year always comes after financial year.

7. Sources of Income →

i. What is Sources of Income ?

→ Sources of Income A person may have various sources of income that can be computed under different heads of Income.

The sources of Income can be categorized under 5 heads :

  • Income from salary :

    → Any income that you receive in terms of the service you provide on a contract of employment is applicable for taxation under this head. This includes salary, advance salary, perquisites, gratuity, commission, annual bonus and pension.

  • Income from House or Property :

    → An individual’s income from his or her property or land is taxable under the head of income from house property. To put it simply, this head includes the policy for calculating tax on rental income that you receive from your properties.

  • Income from Profits and gains of business and professions :

    → The profits that you earn from any kind of business or profession are taxable under this head. You can subtract your expenses from the total income in order to determine the amount on which tax is chargeable.

  • Income from Capital Gains :

    → When you earn profits by transferring or selling an asset that was held as an investment, that income is taxable under the head of income from capital gains. A large number of assets, like gold, bonds, mutual funds, real estate, stocks, etc., fall under capital assets.

  • Income from other sources :

    → Among the five heads of income tax, this one includes any other income that does not have any mention in the above 4 heads. They fall under Section 56 sub-section (2) of the Income Tax Act and include income from lottery, bank deposits, gambling, card games, sports rewards, etc.

It includes income from salary, house property, business or professional income, capital gains and other sources.

The slab system was introduced to maintain a fair tax system in the country. The slabs tend to change at every budget announcement.

15. Education Cess →

i. What is Education Cess ?

→ Education cess is an additional tax levy that is collected over and above a person's basic liability of tax. It is used to fund primary, secondary and higher education in the country.

18. Residential Status →

i. What is Residential Status ?

→ The residential status of a taxpayer is important because the income tax department provides for a different tax treatment for its taxpayers.

Hence, it is imperative for every taxpayer to determine their residential status before doing their tax planning, computing tax payable, and tax filing.

  • There are 2 rules to determine Residential status →

    1. Basic Rule :

    → An individual taxpayer will be considered a resident if they fulfill at least one of the following conditions :

    i. He/ she stays in India for 182 days or more during the financial year.

    ii. He/ she stays in India for 365 days or more during the 4 preceding financial years. In addition, stays for a period of 60 days or more in the relevant financial year.

    2. Additional Rule :

    → An individual taxpayer will be a Ordinarily resident if he/ she fulfills all of the following conditions :

    i. He/ she stays in India for 2 year or more out of 10 years.

    ii. He/ she stays in India for 730 days or more out of 7 years.
